title: Personal Jurisdiction Question

Gerald Mayo sues Satan and his Staff in federal court. In his complaint, Mayo asserts claims for deprivation of his civil rights under [18 U.S.C. § 241](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/241), [28 U.S.C. § 1343](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/1343), and [42 U.S.C. § 1983](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1983), along with claims under state law for assault and intentional infliction of emotional distress. Mayo seeks damages exceeding $75,000 against the defendants jointly and severally.  

Mayo is a lifelong resident of Pennsylvania. Satan and most members of his Staff have never resided in Pennsylvania. However, one member of Satan's Staff does reside in the state. 

Does the court have subject matter jurisdiction?
