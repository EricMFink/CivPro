Gerald Mayo sues Satan and his Staff in federal court. In his complaint, Mayo asserts claims for deprivation of his civil rights under [18 U.S.C. § 241](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/18/241), [28 U.S.C. § 1343](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/28/1343), and [42 U.S.C. § 1983](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1983), along with claims under state law for assault and intentional infliction of emotional distress. Mayo seeks $100,000 in compensatory and punitive damages against the defendants jointly and severally.  

Mayo is a lifelong resident of Pennsylvania. Satan has never resided in Pennsylvania. However, one member of Satan's Staff does reside in the state. 

_Does the court have subject matter jurisdiction?_
